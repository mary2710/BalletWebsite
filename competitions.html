Ballet Competitions Around the World
There are many different international Ballet Competitions, but here are some of the most well known/most prestigious:
  • Youth America Grand Prix (YAGP)
  • Prix de Lausanne
  • International Ballet Competition (IBC)

YAGP
The Youth America Grand Prix, or YAGP, is one of, if not the most well known ballet competition in the world and has been held every year since 1999. It has two rounds, the Regional Finals and the Finals. The Regional Finals take place in differnt locations every year, but this year (2019), it is:
  • Seoul, Korea
  • Sao Paolo, Brazil
  • Melbourne, Australia
  • Brisbane, Australia
  • Jakarta, Indonesia
  • Beijing, China
  • Osaka, Japan
  • Orizaba, Mexico
  • Paris, France
    • >
  • Bishkek, Kyrgyzstan
  • Cattolica, Italy
  • Barcelona, Spain

As well as the International locations, there are a range of Semi-Final competitions in lots of places in America, YAGP's home country.

After the Semi-Final, all contestants who scored 96 points or above are invited to compete in the Finals which take place in New York City, USA.

In the competition, there are 5 types of dance you can perform.
  • Classical Solo
  • Contemporary Solo
  • Classical Pas De Deux
  • Contemporary Pas de Deux
  • Ensemble

It is mandatory for all contestants to perform a classical solo, and there is a list of variations from different ballets that you can choose to perform. In the semi-finals, you don't have to perform a contemporary solo as well, but you must perform one in addition to the classical variation if you make it the the finals.

A Pas de Deux is the ballet name for a duet, literally meaning "dance of two". It is not mandatory to perform one of these, and if you choose to do so, it has to be in addition to your clasical solo. If you perform a pas de deux, you have to do a classical one, again there are a list of dances you can perform from different ballets. It is also possible to perform a contemporary pas de deux in addition to your classical pas de deux.

An ensemble is a group dance. This can either be classical, contemporary, or somewhere in between.

Prix de Lausanne
The Prix de Lausanne competition is held in January of every year in Lausanne, Switzerland. Around 80 candidates from 30 countries around the world aged 15-18 compete in the first round, hoping to be one of the lucky 20 dancers selected for the finals. The aim of the competition is for the winner to embark on their professional career in ballet.
