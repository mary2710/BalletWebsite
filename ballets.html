Different Ballets

There are many different ballets performed around the world but here is a bit about some of the most famous:

The Nutcracker

The Nutcracker is arguably the most well known ballet in the world. It tells the story of Clara (sometimes called Marie), who is at a Christmas party with her family. She recieves a Nutcracker doll as her Christmas present and goes to sleep. She has a dream in which she is transported to a magical realm (called the Land of Sweets) with her Nutcracker. There, she meets the Sugar Plum Fairy, who helps her through the land. Some dances with international themes are performed, as a metaphor for the food she is given. They are:

  • Spanish, which represents Chocolate
  • Arabian, which represents Coffee
  • Chinese, which represents Tea
  • Russian, which represents Candy Canes
  • French, which represents Marzipan

After this, the corps de ballet perform the Waltz of the Flowers, and then everyone comes together to dance. This marks the end of the show.

Swan Lake

Swan Lake tells the story of Prince Siegfred and a White swan named Odette. The ballet begins as Prince Siegfred is at his 21st Birthday party, where he receives a crossbow to symbolise his future responsibilities. He ends up running away to the woods because he does not want to be responsible. He finds himself at a magical lake which is where he meets the swan queen, Odette. She tells him about an evil sorcerer named Von Rothbart, who has turns all of the girls into swans and happens to be in disguise as Prince Siegfred's mentor. The next day, Prince Siegfred meets many prospective princesses, although he can't stop thinking about Odette. Von Rothbart, the sorcerer then arrives and brings his daughter, Odile. Although, he has cast a spell on her to be disguised as Odette. Odette and Odile is usually portrayed by the same principal dancer. The Prince is captivated by her and dances with her. This is where the iconic dance where Odile performs 32 fouette turns en pointe happens. Although, Prince Siegfred doesn't know that the real Odette is watching from the window. The prince confesses his love to Odile, thinking that she is Odette. Horrified, Odette runs away from the window and Prince Siegfried notices her. He leaves the party and chases after Odette. She has fled to the lake and joins the other girls in sadness. Prince Siegfred arrives and tells Odette that Von Rothbart tricked him. She forgives him. Soon after, Von Rothbart and Odile arrive in their magical, bird-like forms. Von Rothbart tells the Prince that he must stick to his word and marry his daughter and they begin to argue. Prince Siegfried tells Von Rothbart that he would rather die with Odette than marry Odile. Prince Siegfried and Odette jump into the lake and they drown. This breaks the spell and all the remaining swans turn back into humans and push Von Rothbart and Odile into the lake, causing them to drown too. The girls watch as the spirits of Prince Siegfried and Odette ascend into heaven.


Don Quixote

Don Quixote tells the story of Don Quixote. He and his servant Sancho Panza set out to have an adventure. They meet Kitri and Basilio in Barcelona. They are a young couple who cannot marry because Kitri's father wants to marry her off a wealthy man named Gamache and Don Quixote decides to intervene. With the help of Don Quixote, Kitri and Basilio manage to persude her father into letting them marry. They also persuade Gamache that he would rather marry the barmaid anyway.
